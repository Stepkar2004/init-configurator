# voice — writing mechanics for anything a stranger reads

> Public, generic layer: punctuation and manner defaults for posts, outreach, and other
> reader-facing text. A specific author's personal voice does NOT live here. See the
> overlay pointer at the bottom.

## The meta-rule: manner, not a word-blacklist

Everything here describes a manner and pattern, with examples. It is not a rulebook of
banned words. "Avoid X" means "the way X usually reads is off," not "the string X is
forbidden." When a draft feels wrong it is almost always a manner mismatch (hedging where
the author would be certain, generic where they would be specific), not one bad word. Fix
the manner. Do not build a blacklist.

## Punctuation

- **No em dashes (—) or en dashes (–), anywhere in reader-facing text.** When a sentence
  reaches for one, split it in two or use a comma. This is the most common reason a draft
  stops sounding human. Compound hyphens are fine and normal ("open-source", "worst-case
  wait", "3-signal corridor"). A hyphen joining a modifier is not a dash.
- **The colon is a reveal, never a label.** Use it to build a phrase and then deliver the
  payoff, the definition, or the list ("The result, over 20 runs:"). Never as a header tag
  like "Context:" or "Role:". A colon introducing a label reads as template copy.
- **No semicolons.** Split into short sentences instead.

## Manner

- **Plain spoken words over jargon.** Explain it to a smart friend, not a committee.
- **Lead with the point.** The first line carries it. Assume no one reads line two.
- **Concrete beats abstract.** Real numbers, real names, real failures, rounded the way
  people actually say them. Never "significantly improved performance".
- **White space is load-bearing.** One idea per paragraph, often one sentence. A key number
  gets its own line.
- **Fragments for emphasis are fine, on purpose.** "Dozens, not hundreds." "A tie."
- **The stranger test.** If this could have been written about any project or any person,
  cut until only this one's version remains.

## Personal voice overlay (private, not public)

How a specific author sounds (self-description, anchor phrases, registers, texture pulled
from their own writing) is personal and never belongs in this synced, public file. If a
`voice.local.md` sits beside this file, load it too and let it override these defaults. It
is the author's own voice: private, never synced across repos, kept out of published
copies. Keep this file generic. Keep the person in the overlay.
